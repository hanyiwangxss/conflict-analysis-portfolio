# Impact of AI Readiness on Modern Drone Warfare Patterns

## 1. Project Overview
This research investigates the relationship between a nation’s **AI Readiness** and its **drone strike modalities** in active conflict zones. By comparing **Ukraine** and **Myanmar** in 2025, the study analyzes how varying levels of technological development and adoption reshape the scale, frequency, and lethality of modern warfare.

## 2. Data Sources
*   **Conflict Event Data**: Extracted from the **ACLED** (Armed Conflict Location & Event Data Project) 2025 dataset. The research focuses on the "Air/drone strike" sub-event type across Europe, Central Asia, and the Asia-Pacific.
*   **AI Readiness Assessment**: Derived from the **Government AI Readiness Index 2025** by Oxford Insights, evaluating nations across six primary pillars of AI capacity.
*   **Theoretical Framework**: Based on the "Hyperwar" and OODA loop collapse theories discussed by Amir Husain in *PRISM 9, No. 3*, and James Johnson’s research on Drone Swarming and Future Warfare.

## 3. Methodology
*   **Spatio-Temporal Analysis**: Visualizing total strike frequencies and monthly evolution trends throughout 2025 for both target countries.
*   **Lethality Evaluation**: Calculating the **fatality rate per strike** to distinguish between high-volume attrition strategies and high-value targeting.
*   **Capability Gap Comparison**: Benchmarking AI Infrastructure and Human Capital scores to assess the technological divide.
*   **Elasticity Analysis**: Employing a proxy of `(log Strike ratio / log Readiness ratio)` to identify which AI pillars act as the most resilient catalysts for scaled warfare.

## 4. Key Findings

### The Ukraine Model: High-Volume & Industrialized Attrition
*   **Scale Effect**: Ukraine demonstrated absolute dominance with a strike volume nearly **nine times** that of Myanmar. Drones are treated as "attritable systems"—low-cost, high-volume assets used to overwhelm defenses.
*   **Low Lethality per Event**: With a fatality rate of only **0.16 per strike**, the objective is mass pressure and psychological suppression rather than maximizing casualties in every single engagement.

### The Myanmar Model: Low-Volume & High-Value Targeting
*   **Tactical Reliance**: Due to lower AI Sector Maturity, Myanmar relies on self-produced or repurposed agricultural drones.
*   **High Lethality per Event**: Myanmar’s fatality rate is **0.79 per strike** (nearly five times that of Ukraine), indicating that limited drone assets are reserved for high-impact strikes on personnel clusters.

### Elasticity Analysis Results
*   **Infrastructure as Catalyst**: "Enabling Technical Infrastructure" showed the highest elasticity (over 12), suggesting that gains in foundational capacity lead to exponential increases in strike frequency.
*   **The Threshold Effect**: AI readiness does not influence warfare linearly; crossing a specific technical threshold allows a nation to transition from improvised use to algorithmically coordinated warfare.

## 5. Limitations
*   **Dataset Noise**: The ACLED "Air/drone strike" category includes traditional manned aircraft, which may introduce non-AI-related kinetic data.
*   **Autonomy Differentiation**: The data aggregates simple Remotely Piloted Vehicles (RPVs) with advanced Autonomous Weapon Systems (AWS) without distinguishing specific levels of autonomy.
